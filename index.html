About tastingnotes
Telling the story of wine

It is fascinating how a tiny berry, with the right guidance of dedicated people, and with the help of nature and time, turns into this magical product that we call wine. And the most fascinating of it all, you read all this in your glass: time, effort, nature, sun, rain, air, attention and dedication.

Coups de Coeur in Brussels ❤️

My favorite wine places in Brussels? A night out in the town often starts at Rubis in Saint-Gilles. First I frequented the place because a lot of my friends lived in the neighborhood, but soon we just met there for the place itself. The thing about Rubis is, it is a generous place. You can give a description of what you feel like drinking, and the friendly personnel gives you a few options to taste before choosing your wine.

Tasting notes on Lanzarote wines

Lanzarote is one of the Canary Islands and after Tenerife the biggest producer of wines of the region. These wines rarely make it to our shops though. Firstly of all because they do not have an outstanding reputation. But being on the island, I did find some very nice quality wines. Wine makers embraced modern techniques together with some very old craftmanship. I personally fell for the wines of the bodega Bodega Vulcano de Lanzarote.
